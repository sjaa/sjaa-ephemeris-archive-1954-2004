**SPECIAL NOTE:** There will be no time for much news this month in order that the Bulletin can be mailed early.

**JUST A FEW ITEMS:** (1) The constitution amendments were passed at the meeting of Sept. 5, 1975.

(2) THIS WILL BE THE LAST MAILING TO MEMBERS WHO DID NOT RENEW THEIR MEMBERSHIP FOR 1975-1976.

(3) **Correction:** The first comet picture was taken by Gerry Rattley. Jack Zeiders took the first color shot.

(4) Celestial music floated over it. Unanhum at the last star party.

(5) Your editor may not have been the first in the world to report Nova Cygni 1975, but he was the first on the Pacific coast. (Ranking the blowing of my own horn but this is my only claim to fame.)

(6) See Don Alber's article in the latest Mercury Magazine (ASP).

### COMING EVENTS:

**Oct. 3, 1975. 7:30 P.M. at Linder Center.** Space Colonization talk delivered by Dr. Soins, executive director of the Astronomical Society of the Pacific and editor of Mercury Magazine. (See flyer.)

**Oct. 4, 1975. at Henry Coe State Park.** Star party.

**Oct. 10, 1975. 8 P.M. at Dr. Gregory's home:** Board meeting.

**Oct. 11, 1975. Mt. Umunhum.** First quarter star party.

### ANOTHER ITEM:

Juna Hild has been deeded a lovely sao-side chalet on Mare Imbrium. Juna donated a photographic albunum to the club's library.

The club's library welcomes astro-pix. So far we have two Milky-Way gems taken by Jack Zeiders.
