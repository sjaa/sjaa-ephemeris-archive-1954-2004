# WAA REPORT

The annual business meeting of the Western Amateur Astronomers was hold at the Hacienda Motor Hotel in Fresno, California on Saturday, 17 January, 1970. Twenty societies were represented; the meeting began at 8PM, after an informal dinner.

Under old business was pending the matter of disputing the closing of the Lick Observatory public observing program. WAA president Arthur Leonard read an answer he had received from Dr Robert Kraft, acting director of Lick, in response to a letter of inquiry. His report was supplemented by a four-page statement composed, and read in part, by TB Billi both efforts were well-received, and further action was deemed inappropriate.

New business: Martin Rohrke of Sacramento Valley Astronomical Society distributed lists of hotels and motels in Sacramento near the Mansion Inn (16th and H Streets) where the August 19-22, 1970 convention will be held.* A call for papers should appear within the next 60 days; WAA paper chairmen is Clifford Holmes, 8642 Wells, Riverside, Calif. 92509. Papers should be camera-ready. Also soon will appear detailed registration information and a list of restaurants. The recipient of the 1970 G Bruce Blair award was elected; his name will be revealed in the May issue of Sky and Telescope.

However, four months before the convention there will be two smaller conferences: 18-19 April, 1970--Telescope Makers Conference, Riverside, sponsored jointly by the Riverside Astronomical Society and the Riverside City College. Featured will be a competition for the optical and mechanical qualities of the amateur telescope. Speakers will be:

Robert Cox, Sky and Telescope ATM Editor  
Arthur Leonard, UC Davis  
Richard Buchroeder, U of Arizona, Tucson  
James Kent, Citrus College Optical Department  
Leland Barnes, Cal Astro Optics  
Robert Dixon, Planetarium Director, RCC  
Victor Nikolashin, U of Arizona, Tucson  
John Lytle, Hughes, Tucson

Full details will come in a future bulletin.

25 April, 1970--This very next weekend is the third annual Bay Area Astronomical Meeting, to be held at the Marin County Civic Center, 15 miles north of San Rafael, off Highway 101. There will be an optional dinner 5PM to 6PM, and the meeting itself is at 6PM, featuring speakers from UC Berkeley. Afterwards, weather permitting, will be a starparty. For details (and dinner-notification to be sent before April 18) write to Mal Beck, Secretary, Star Observers of Marin, 170 Hillside Ave, San Rafael, Calif, 94901.

The 1971 telescope makers conference will tentatively be held in northern California. The 1971 WAA convention will meet in Hawaii, and the 1972 convention has a tentative bid from Riverside.
